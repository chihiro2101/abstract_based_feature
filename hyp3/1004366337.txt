image : jerash bw 12.jpg thumb 300px the oval forum and cardo of gerasa ( jerash ) the names of the traditional ten cities of the decapolis come from natural history ( pliny ) except for damascus , the decapolis cities were by and large founded during the hellenistic period , between the death of alexander the great in 323 bc and the roman conquest of coele-syria , including judea in 63 bc the cities were greek from their founding , modeling themselves on the greek polis the decapolis was a region where two cultures interacted : the culture of the greek colonists and the indigenous semitic culture at the same time , cultural blending and borrowing also occurred in the decapolis region the cities acted as centers for the diffusion of greek culture when pompey reorganized the region , he awarded a group of these cities with autonomy under roman protection for centuries the cities based their calendar era on this conquest : 63 bc was the epochal year of the pompeian era , used to count the years throughout the roman and byzantine periods the imperial cult , the worship of the roman emperor , was a very common practice throughout the decapolis and was one of the features that linked the different cities the new testament gospels of matthew , mark , and luke mention that the decapolis region was a location of the ministry of jesus the decapolis came under direct roman rule in ad 106 , when arabia petraea was annexed during the reign of the emperor trajan the roman and byzantine decapolis region was influenced and gradually taken over by christianity
