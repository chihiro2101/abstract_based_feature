stalag 17 begins on '' the longest night of the year '' in december 1944 in a stalag somewhere along the danube the camp holds poles , czechs , russian women and , in the american compound , 640 sergeants from bomber crews , gunners , radiomen , and flight engineers sefton , an enterprising cynic who barters openly with the german guards for eggs , silk stockings , blankets and other luxuries sefton tells the men it is foolish to try to escape they use a clandestine radio , smuggled from barracks to barracks , to pick up the bbc and the war news dunbar admitted to the other prisoners that he had blown up a passing german ammunition train while he was being transported to the camp sefton resents dunbar for coming from a wealthy boston family and accuses him of having used his family 's fortune to make it through officer candidate school while sefton washed out on christmas day , the men find out that ss men are coming to take dunbar to berlin for his sabotage sefton asks him , '' when was pearl harbor ? '' price knows the date , but sefton quickly asks what time he heard the news the film was adapted by wilder and edwin blum from the broadway play by donald bevan and edmund trzcinski which was based on their experiences as prisoners in stalag 17b in austria trzcinski appears in the film as a prisoner the character sefton was loosely based on joe palazzo , a flier in trzcinski 's prisoner-of-war barracks peter graves recalled the film was held from release for over a year due to paramount pictures not believing anyone would be interested in seeing a film about prisoners of war the film was well received and is considered , along with the great escape and the bridge on the river kwai ( also starring holden ) , among the best world war ii prisoner of war films an uncredited william holden reprised the character of a cigar-chewing pow in the 1979 war movie escape to athena
