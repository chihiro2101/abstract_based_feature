stalag 17 begins on '' the longest night of the year '' in december 1944 in a stalag somewhere along the danube the camp holds poles , czechs , russian women and , in the american compound , 640 sergeants from bomber crews , gunners , radiomen , and flight engineers sefton , an enterprising cynic who barters openly with the german guards for eggs , silk stockings , blankets and other luxuries sefton tells the men it is foolish to try to escape sefton bribes the guards to let him spend the day in the russian women 's barracks dunbar admitted to the other prisoners that he had blown up a passing german ammunition train while he was being transported to the camp on christmas day , the men find out that ss men are coming to take dunbar to berlin for his sabotage after von scherbach threatens to raze the camp if necessary , the men decide one of them must get dunbar out sefton asks him , '' when was pearl harbor ? '' price knows the date , but sefton quickly asks what time he heard the news sefton decides to take dunbar out himself because he likes the odds and the expected reward from dunbar 's family the men give sefton enough time to get dunbar out of the water tower above one of the latrines , then throw price out into the yard with tin cans tied to his legs the film was adapted by wilder and edwin blum from the broadway play by donald bevan and edmund trzcinski which was based on their experiences as prisoners in stalag 17b in austria the character sefton was loosely based on joe palazzo , a flier in trzcinski 's prisoner-of-war barracks peter graves recalled the film was held from release for over a year due to paramount pictures not believing anyone would be interested in seeing a film about prisoners of war an uncredited william holden reprised the character of a cigar-chewing pow in the 1979 war movie escape to athena
