species of aspidistra are perennial herbaceous plants growing from rhizomes they arise more or less directly from the rhizome , rather than being borne on stems the flowering stem ( scape ) is usually very short so that the flowers appear low down among the leaves the genus aspidistra was named by the english botanist john ker gawler in 1822 , as a blend of greek ασπίς/ασπίδ- aspid- and the genus species of aspidistra are part of the ground flora , growing in forests and under shrubs , in areas of high rainfall , from eastern india , indochina and china through to japan '' the biggest aspidistra in the world '' aspidistras can withstand deep shade , neglect , dry soil , hot temperatures and polluted in-door air ( from burning coal or natural gas ) but are sensitive to bright
