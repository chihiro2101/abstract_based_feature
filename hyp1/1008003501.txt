butt was born in 1813 in glenfin , a district bordering the finn valley in county donegal in ulster , the northern province in ireland whilst there he co-founded the dublin university magazine and edited it for four years for much of his life he was a member of the irish conservative party , and he founded the conservative ulster times newspaper he was known for his opposition to the irish nationalism leader daniel o'connell 's campaign for the repeal of the act of union he also lectured at trinity college , dublin , in political economy this led to his involvement in irish nationalist politics and the foundation of the home rule league the failed fenian rising in 1867 strengthened butt 's belief that a federal system was the only way to break the dreary cycle of inefficient administration punctuated by incompetent uprisings in 1870 butt then founded the home government association '' he believed that home rule would promote friendship between ireland and her neighbour to the east in november 1873 butt replaced the association with a new body , the home rule league , which he regarded as a pressure-group , rather than a political party in the general election the following year , 60 of its members were elected , forming then in 1874 the irish parliamentary party when parnell entered parliament he took his cue from john o'connor power and joseph biggar and allied himself with those irish members who would support him in his obstructionist campaign butt considered this discussion too important to the british empire to be interrupted by obstructionism and publicly warned the irish members to refrain from this tactic he was fiercely denounced by the young nationalist john dillon , who continued his attacks with considerable support from other home
