decadence was the name given to a number of late nineteenth-century writers who valued artifice over the earlier romantics ' naïve view of nature this concept of decadence dates from the eighteenth century , especially from montesquieu , and was taken up by critics as a term of abuse after désiré nisard used it against victor hugo and romanticism in general in britain and ireland the leading figure associated with the decadent movement was irish writer , oscar wilde the historian jacques barzun ( 1907-2012 ) gives a definition of decadence which is independent from moral judgement in his bestseller from dawn to decadence : 500 years of western cultural life barzun , jacques : from dawn to decadence : 500 years of western cultural life douthat sees the west in the 21st century in an '' age of decadence '' , marked by stalemate and stagnation one who directly opposed the idea of decadence as expressed by lenin was josé ortega y gasset in the revolt of the masses ( 1930 ) he also argued that contemporary progress was opposite the true decadence of the roman empire decadence is an important aspect of contemporary left communism theory collection of the articles of the international communist current on the theory of decadence chronology of decadence decadence , symbolist , and the fin de siècle : a notebook
