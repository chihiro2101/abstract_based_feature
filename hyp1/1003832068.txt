yawl rig appears to have originated in the early 19th century john macgregor had his 1867-built rob roy designed as a yawl they remained prominent in handicap classes through the 1920s - with yachts like rendezvous ( built 1913 ) measuring and setting of sail before '' yawl '' became the name of a rig , it was a hull type generally , a yawl is a double-ended , clinker built open boat , which can be worked under sail or oar most were operated from a beach or a small harbour , with the boat being hauled out of the water when not in use there are examples in both shetland and orkney , with type and local variations in design the shetland boats include foureens and sixareens - the names denote the number of oars they were designed to use until the 19th century , most of these boats were built in norway and then transported to shetland disassembled , where they were put back together again surviving examples and a replica of some of these shetland boats are in the shetland museum the norfolk and suffolk beach yawls were probably the fastest open boats ever built the fastest type of beach yawl was used for taking pilots and passengers out to ships some would be continuously baling to rid the boat of the spray that came aboard the size of an individual yawl would vary depending on the size of the ship to which she belonged - though the yawl was usually the smallest of the various boats carried
