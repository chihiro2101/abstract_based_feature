xanthippe means '' yellow horse '' , from '' blond '' and '' horse '' also , hippeis , literally '' horsemen '' or '' knights '' , was the name of one of the highest socio-economic classes of athens xanthippe 's father is believed to have been named lamprocles if he was even more well-established in athenian aristocracy than was socrates ' father , his name would have been the preferred choice for the name of the first-born son plato 's portrayal of xanthippe in the phaedo suggests that she was a devoted wife and mother ; plato phaedo , 60a–b , 116b she is mentioned nowhere else in plato xenophon , in his memorabilia , portrays her in much the same light , although he does make lamprocles complain of her harshness xenophon , symposium 17–19 2.10 perhaps this picture of xanthippe originated with the historical antisthenes , one of socrates ' pupils , since xenophon initially puts this view into his mouth if you will get for yourself a good wife , you will be happy forever after ; and if by chance you will get a common scold like my xanthippe — why then you will become a philosopher in william shakespeare 's the taming of the shrew , petruchio compares katherina '' as socrates ' xanthippe or a worse '' in act 1 scene 2 a fictional account of xanthippe 's relationship with her husband is presented in the play xanthippe by the british author and playwright deborah freeman a species of african white-toothed shrew was described by wilfred hudson osgood in 1910 as crocidura xantippe , common name '' xanthippe 's shrew
