thompson was born in rural woburn , massachusetts , on march 26 , 1753 ; his birthplace is preserved as a museum thompson excelled at his trade , and coming in contact with refined and well educated people for the first time , adopted many of their characteristics including an interest in science while recuperating in woburn in 1769 from an injury , thompson conducted experiments concerning the nature of heat and began to correspond with loammi baldwin and others about them her father was a minister , and her late husband left her property at concord , new hampshire , then called rumford they moved to portsmouth , new hampshire , and through his wife 's influence with the governor , he was appointed a major in the new hampshire militia image : count rumford.jpg right thumb painting by thomas gainsborough 1783 when the american revolutionary war began thompson was a man of property and standing in new england and was opposed to the uprising thompson was welcomed by the british to whom he gave valuable information about the american forces , and became an advisor to both thomas gage and lord george germain while working with the british armies in america he conducted experiments to measure the force of gunpowder , the results of which were widely acclaimed when published in 1781 in the philosophical transactions of the royal society he also invented rumford 's soup , a soup for the poor , and established the cultivation of the potato in bavaria he then made the somewhat reckless , and incorrect , inference that air and , in fact , all gases , were perfect non-conductors of heat rumford had observed the frictional heat generated by boring cannon at the arsenal in munich rumford argued that the seemingly indefinite generation of heat was incompatible with the caloric theory rumford made no attempt to further quantify the heat generated or to measure the mechanical equivalent of heat he did experiments to support his theories of calorific and frigorific radiation and said the communication of heat was the net effect of calorific ( hot ) rays and frigorific ( cold ) rays and the rays emitted by the object his work was also very profitable , and much imitated when he published his analysis of the way chimneys worked the retention of heat was a recurring theme in his work , as he is also credited with the invention of thermal underwear thompson endowed the rumford medals of the royal society and the american academy of arts and sciences , and endowed a professorship at harvard university collected works of count rumford , volume i ,
