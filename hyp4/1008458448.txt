a hill fort has existed in talsi at least since the 10th century , originally inhabited by curonians during the northern crusades , the settlement came under german over-lordship and a castle was built in talsi during the late 13th century the settlement grew in the 15th century , when traders and artisans from german-speaking lands settled in talsi the town became part of the russian empire in 1795 together with the rest of courland at that time , baltic germans constituted about sixty percent of the population at the end of the 18th century , jews were allowed to settle in talsi for the first time most of the jews who settled in talsi originally came from lithuania and would with time come to form a substantial part of the town population eventually a synagogue was built in talsi the town had a forceful leader in kārlis grīnbergs , chairman of talsi executive committee ( as the town now was part of the latvian soviet socialist republic under soviet occupation ) this hill has an arboretum and is the location of talsi regional museum , housed in a manor house originally built for a baltic nobility , baron von firck talsi lake , in the middle of the town , is surrounded by a promenade and there is a fountain installed in the lake there is also a cultural centre with a '' creative yard '' in the middle of the town
