a hill fort has existed in talsi at least since the 10th century , originally inhabited by curonians during the northern crusades , the settlement came under german over-lordship and a castle was built in talsi during the late 13th century the settlement grew in the 15th century , when traders and artisans from german-speaking lands settled in talsi during the reign of duke jacob kettler , an iron smelting furnace was constructed in talsi , an early industrial development at the end of the 18th century , jews were allowed to settle in talsi for the first time most of the jews who settled in talsi originally came from lithuania and would with time come to form a substantial part of the town population in 1887 a russian-language school was also opened in talsi during the revolution of 1905 , there was unrest in the town and several houses burned down and inhabitants killed peace was not entirely restored until 1920 , with the end of the latvian war of independence which made talsi part of the newly proclaimed latvian republic the town had a forceful leader in kārlis grīnbergs , chairman of talsi executive committee ( as the town now was part of the latvian soviet socialist republic under soviet occupation ) the highest hill in talsi is tiguļu kalns this hill has an arboretum and is the location of talsi regional museum , housed in a manor house originally built for a baltic nobility , baron von firck
