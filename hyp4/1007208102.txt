hobson joined the royal navy on 25 august 1803 as a second-class volunteer he served in the napoleonic wars and was later involved in the suppression of piracy in the caribbean between march 1823 and may 1824 he commanded the schooner , in which he captured several pirate vessels , earning himself the nickname '' lion hobson '' in december 1834 he obtained a commission from the first lord of the admiralty - george eden , 1st earl of auckland - to the east indies on on 18 september 1836 , left for port phillip district ( later melbourne ) conveying captain william lonsdale and other officials to the new colony during the next three months , hobson and his officers thoroughly surveyed port phillip , the northern portion of which , by direction of governor sir richard bourke , was named hobsons bay , after him on 26 may 1837 hobson sailed to the bay of islands , new zealand , in response to a request for help from james busby , the british resident , who felt threatened by wars between māori tribes on his return to england in 1838 , hobson submitted a report on new zealand , in which he proposed establishing british sovereignty over the islands in small pockets similar to the hudson 's bay company in canada the treaty of waitangi was first proposed by hobson on his return to britain from his first visit to new zealand hobson was sworn in by the chief justice as governor of new zealand on 3 may 1841 hobson faced opposition from the '' senate clique '' radicals who sent a petition to the foreign secretary to have hobson recalled correspondence and other papers relating to new zealand , 1835–1845
