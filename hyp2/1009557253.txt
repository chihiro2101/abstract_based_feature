brett gurewitz formed epitaph records as a vehicle for releases by his band bad religion its first release for the label was bad religion 's 1981 self-titled ep , followed by their debut how could hell be any worse ? , which was also the label 's first full-length release two more bad religion releases followed – into the unknown and the ep back to the known – before their temporary split in 1989 , gurewitz signed nofx to epitaph this was followed by bad religion 's next two albums – no control and against the grain – which sold 60,000 and 100,000 copies respectively by 1993 , more punk acts had signed to epitaph , including pennywise , down by law , coffin break , the offspring , rancid , rkl , snfu , total chaos and claw hammer although bad religion was the founding band of epitaph , releasing their early records through the label , they switched over to atlantic in 1993 , with recipe for hate being their first record outside of the label that year bad religion and epitaph received widespread fame , both within and outside the punk community , when bad religion ( even though they had left epitaph by this time ) , nofx , rancid and the offspring all released hit records in 2001 , brett gurewitz returned to bad religion , and the band returned to epitaph records , piccoli , sean ( 2002 ) '' punk 's been good to bad religion '' , south florida sun-sentinel , march 1 , 2002 , p epitaph signed weezer in 2010 , the label releasing hurley later that year
