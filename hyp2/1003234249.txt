born in 1898 , bäke volunteered for the german army in may 1915 and was posted to an infantry regiment from 1919 to 1921 , bäke served in the freikorps epp , a right-wing paramilitary unit named after franz ritter von epp bäke 's unit took part in the invasion of poland as part of the 1st light division , which was redesignated 6th panzer division in october 1939 following the encirclement of the german 6th army at stalingrad , the division took part in the abortive attempt to relieve the 6th army in operation winter storm in december 1942 and then retreated to kharkov in january 1943 , bäke was awarded the knight 's cross of the iron cross for his actions during operation citadel , bäke was awarded the oak leaves to the knight 's cross bäke single-handedly destroyed three soviet tanks during the battle with infantry weapons at close range , for which he received three tank destruction badges bäke 's command found itself poorly deployed and under sustained counter-attack from american infantry on 10 march he was appointed commander of panzer division feldherrnhalle 2 , formally the 13th panzer division , and sent to hungary bäke is one of the '' panzer aces '' , that is , highly decorated tank commanders popularised by the german author franz kurowski in his 1992 book panzer aces , along with kurt knispel and michael wittmann during world war ii , bäke participated in over 400 tank combat missions , 13 of which resulted in the destruction of his tank
