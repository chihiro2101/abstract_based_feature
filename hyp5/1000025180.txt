see also : spanish dollar , spanish real the name peso was given to the 8-real silver coin introduced in 1497 , minted at 8 pesos to a castilian mark ( 230.0465 grams ) of silver 134/144 fine ( 25.56 g fine silver ) it was minted in large quantities after the discovery of silver in mexico , peru and bolivia in the 16th century and immediately became a coin of worldwide importance in international trade between europe , asia and north america initially the peso was produced in spanish latin america in a rapid and simplified manner by cutting off a lump of silver of proper weight and fineness from the end of a silver bar , which was then flattened out and impressed by a hammer there was a simultaneous reduction in weight and fineness to 8.5 pesos to a mark ( 27.064 g ) , 0.9167 fine or 24.809 g fine silver this coin was known to english colonists in north america as a piece of eight , then later on as a spanish dollar , spanish milled dollar , and finally as a mexican dollar a sample of coins at the end of the 18th century , however , confirm a fineness of only 0.896 ( hence , fine silver 24.25 g ) ; see currency of spanish america coinage of 1786 in 1686 spain minted a coin worth 8 reales provinciales ( or only 0.80 , known as the peso maria or peso sencillo ) which was poorly received by the people the philippine peso ( filipino : piso ) is derived from the spanish silver coin real de a ocho or spanish dollar , in wide circulation in the entire america and southeast asia during the 17th and 18th centuries , through its use in the spanish colonies and even in the united states and canada thus , following the great silver devaluation of 1873 , the philippine peso devalued in parallel with the mexican unit , and by the end of the 19th century , was worth half a united states dollar
