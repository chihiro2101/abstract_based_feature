praefectus , often with a further qualification , was the formal title of many , fairly low to high-ranking , military or civil officials in the roman empire , whose authority was not embodied in their person ( as it was with elected magistrates ) but conferred by delegation from a higher authority the praetorian prefect ( praefectus praetorio ) began as the military commander of a general 's guard company in the field , then grew in importance as the praetorian guard became a potential kingmaker during the empire praefectus urbi , or praefectus urbanus : city prefect , in charge of the administration of rome praefectus cohortus : commander of a cohort ( constituent unit of a legion , or analogous unit ) praefectus orae maritimae : official in charge with the control and defense of an important sector of sea coast for some auxiliary troops , specific titles could even refer to their peoples : praefectus laetorum ( germanic , notably in gaul ) praefectus sarmatarum gentilium (
