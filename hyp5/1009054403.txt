officials for the state 's bureaucracy this system had a huge influence on both society and culture in imperial china and was directly responsible for the creation of a class of scholar-bureaucrats irrespective of their family pedigree originally appointments to the bureaucracy were based on the patronage of aristocrats ; during han dynasty , emperor wu of han established the xiaolian system of recommendation by superiors for appointments to office this was an early form of the imperial examinations , transitioning from inheritance and patronage to merit , in which local officials would select candidates to take part in an examination of the confucian classics after the fall of the han dynasty , the chinese bureaucracy regressed into a semi-merit system known as the nine-rank system
