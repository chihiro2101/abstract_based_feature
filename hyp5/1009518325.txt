the second temple period the second temple period is the period in ancient israel between the construction of the second temple in jerusalem in 516 bce and its destruction by the romans in 70 ce throughout the second temple period , jerusalem saw several shifts in rule alexander 's conquest of the mediterranean world brought an end to persian control of jerusalem ( 539 bce–334/333 bce ) and ushered in the hellenistic period the hellenistic period , which extended from 334/333 bce to 63 bce , is known today for the spread of hellenistic influence after the death of alexander in 323 bce , his generals divided the empire among themselves and for the next 30 years , they fought for control of the empire antiochus iv epiphanes of syria , a seleucid , disrupted whatever peace there had been in judea when he desecrated the temple in jerusalem and forced jews to violate the torah though the maccabees rebelled against the seleucids in 164 bce , seleucid rule did not end for another 20 years after a few years of conflict , the romans retook jerusalem and destroyed the temple , bringing an end to the second temple period in 70 ce during the persian period , the temple became more than the center of worship in judea after its reconstruction in 516 bce ;
